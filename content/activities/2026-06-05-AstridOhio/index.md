ChinaComx PhD Candidate [Astrid Y. Xiao](https://chinacomx.github.io/team/astrid/) will present a paper at the [Comics Studies Society (CSS) Annual Conference 2026](https://comicsstudies.org/css-2026/). The theme of the conference this year is __Everything I Know, I Learned From Comics__. The conference will be held at the [Billy Ireland Cartoon Library & Museum](https://cartoons.osu.edu/), [The Ohio State University](https://www.osu.edu/) from __June 11-13__, __2026__. It brings together scholars, educators, librarians, artists, and practitioners to explore how comics shape teaching and learning across different educational and cultural contexts.

As part of __Panel 1C__, __“Comics Ecology, Ecology Comics”__ in _Sullivant 225_, Astrid will present __“From Panels to Planet: Chinese Eco-Comics”__ on __Thursday__, __June 11__, from __9:00-10:15 AM ET__. The moderator of this panel is [Kate Kelp-Stebbins](https://cas.uoregon.edu/directory/profiles/all/kkelp), [University of Oregon](https://www.uoregon.edu/?utm_source=banner-module&utm_campaign=banner). Astrid’s paper examines the ideological and narrative potential of _lianhuanhua_ as a visual narrative form for cultivating ecological consciousness. She traces how these comics rendered environmental concerns visible and emotionally accessible to young readers, from socialist-era narratives of frugality and resource conservation to post-1980s works that promoted environmental ethics through playful, intertextual storytelling and literary imagination.
