ChinaComx PhD Candidate [Jiu Song](https://chinacomx.github.io/team/jiu/) will head to Hamburg this June to share her research at the international workshop [Entangled Images: Early 20th-Century Chinese Popular Culture and Western Collections](https://arthist.net/archive/51737). Hosted by the [Centre for the Study of Manuscript Cultures (CSMC)](https://www.csmc.uni-hamburg.de/) at the [University of Hamburg](https://www.uni-hamburg.de/en.html) on __11-12 June 2026__, the workshop examines visual media, knowledge production, and collecting practices across China and the West.

Jiu will speak in the panel __“Narrative Entanglements”__ (__13:30-14:30__) on __11 June 2026__. Her presentation, titled “__The Reader Inside the Panels: Shaping and Mobilising Readers in 1950s Lianhuanhua (1954-1956)__”, argues that 1950s _lianhuanhua_ never settled on one steady reader, holding the reader in two places at once: an object to be shaped and a subject to be mobilised, two sides of a single editorial move rather than two stages in time. She first reads works from 1955-1956 that stage children reading, where one posture (a child bent over a book) can mark the model reader or the ruined one, so captions, supporting characters, and graded endings, not the image alone, settle the verdict. She then turns to the _Mass-Creation_ 群众创作 column of _Lianhuanhua Bao_ 连环画报, where some readers, trained into the qualified type, were encouraged to pick up the brush, and their work became models for others to learn and copy. A medium carried over from the Republican period, she argues, was retooled to make its readers.
